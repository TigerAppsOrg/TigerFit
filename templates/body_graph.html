Fitness Terminology

A group of repetitions of a single exercise

A single cycle of lifting and moving through the form of an exercise in a controlled manner

When you reach a point where you cannot physically complete a given rep with the current weight before completing all reps

When you increase your personal one rep max estimation on a given exercise which you did not reach failure, according to the Brzycki formula

A topography of movement designed to maximize safety and muscle strength gains

The maximum amount of weight a person can lift for one repetition (on a given exercise)

Measure of a human's exertion of force on physical objects. Power is best obtained by completing 1-6 reps at a time

Increase in size of skeletal muscle through a growth in size of its component cells. Hypertrophy is best obtained by completing 7-12 reps at a time

The ability of an organism to exert itself and remain active for a long period of time, as well as its ability to resist, withstand, recover from. Endurance is best obtained by completing 13+ reps at a time

The total amount of weight lifted (product of reps and weight)

Fitness Questions

We use the Brzycki formula to make one rep max estimations and weight recommendations pictured below,

with W being weight lifted and r being # reps completed. This estimation loses accuracy as r increases, so we only estimate one-rep maximums for 1-16 reps.

This formula is named after its creator, Matt Brzycki, the current Assistant Director of Campus Recreation at Princeton University. From a given set of an exercise that the user inputs, the number of repetitions and the weight used is inputted into the formula, and the users one rep maximum estimate for that exercise is calculated.

First, you have to identify the reason for working out. Based off of this, there are different rep ranges for different purposes (defined in the terminology section above): power, endurance and hypertrophy. Power is worked on by doing sets of 1-6 reps. Hypertrophy can be obtained through exercises consisting of sets of 7-12 reps. Endurance is worked on by doing sets of 13+ reps.

Training to failure, or failing a set, should be done only if you want to achieve hypertrophy. It should also be done sparingly, usually on the last set of a exercise, if at all.

If you have just failed a set, take some time for a short rest and try again, always remember to let your body recover!
